## Resolution

root_cause: |
  The 10-vs-14 width-ordering FLAG's x=70-100mm localization is an EXTRACTION ARTIFACT, not genuine physical bead narrowing. Track 10's raw (pre-detrend) within-column bead contrast (peak-baseline separation) stays flat at ~0.035-0.05mm across the entire 20-100mm scan -- it does not decay toward the noise floor in the 70-100mm region, ruling out a genuine material/process narrowing. Instead, track 10's raw data carries an enormous (~1.7mm, ~40-85x larger than tracks 8/14/21) along-track baseline drift that the shared DETREND_POLY_ORDER=4/DETREND_MAX_Y_DEGREE=2 surface fit must almost entirely reproduce (consistent with 01-11-DIAGNOSIS's independent fitted_span/raw_span~1.01 finding). The fit's own y-shape prediction error (measured directly in the fitted plane itself, isolated from residual noise) grows systematically and monotonically as x approaches the domain's far edge (x=100mm) -- a Runge-type polynomial edge-of-domain effect, the same conceptual risk 01-11-CRITERION named for the y-dimension (leading to the max_y_degree=2 cap), but here occurring along x, which was left uncapped. Because track 10's absolute dynamic range is so much larger than the other tracks', this small but growing fitting-error becomes large enough in absolute mm terms (reaching ~0.01-0.04mm by x=90-99mm) to rival and then exceed track 10's own true bead contrast specifically in the tail, manufacturing an apparent low-y (near-strip-edge) feature in the detrended residual while simultaneously eroding (and eventually over-subtracting) the residual at the true bead's location. This raises the per-column relative half-max threshold (computed independently per column from that column's own 5th/95th percentiles) until the true, still-physically-present bead signal falls below it and its above-threshold run shrinks to a few points or disappears from the candidate set entirely -- which halfmax_edges' boundary-clip exclusion and continuity tracking then correctly (per their existing, unmodified contract) either reject as edge-touching or fail to find a competing interior candidate for. Track 14 shows no comparable tail-localized artifact (its low-y/mid-y residual gap stays small and sign-oscillating with no trend across the full x-range) because its raw dynamic range is ~40x smaller, leaving comfortable margin between the fit's small absolute error and its own bead signal everywhere in x. 01-11-CRITERION's fix-validation test (fitted-surface row-median at each y-strip-edge vs the interior midpoint) is a TRACK-WIDE, all-x-aggregated metric; it does not and cannot detect an artifact concentrated in only the last ~30mm of the 80mm scan, which is why Amendment A5 cleared that criterion in aggregate while this x-localized tail effect persisted.
