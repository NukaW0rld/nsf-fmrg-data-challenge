<objective>
Close UAT gaps G-01-2 and G-01-3, both localized to `src/targets.py`'s boundary-detection and smoothing functions. G-01-2: `halfmax_edges()` (`src/targets.py:99-141`) picks each column's boundary as the single largest above-half-max-threshold run, completely independently per column, with zero cross-column continuity — because 98-100% of valid columns on every real track contain multiple above-threshold blobs of similar magnitude (bead + substrate texture), the "winning" blob flips unpredictably column to column, producing the reported sawtooth/EKG boundary behavior. G-01-3: `nan_savgol()` (`src/targets.py:144-162`) silently becomes a zero-residual-degrees-of-freedom exact interpolant whenever a smoothing window has exactly `SG_POLYORDER+1` (3) finite points — the specific mechanism behind Track 10's reported crop-edge terminal spike.

Purpose: the reviewer confirmed both defects are algorithm-design gaps, not implementation bugs against the locked D-01-D-16/A1/A2 contract (all 14 mechanical contract tests already pass). Fixing them requires a cross-column continuity constraint in edge selection (not a new smoothing pass) and a corrected degree-selection formula in the existing smoother — the smallest correct fixes that directly target each confirmed root cause, not a broader rewrite.

Output: `src/targets.py` with a new `all_true_runs` helper, a continuity-tracked `halfmax_edges` with an explicit stale-history reset after long invalid gaps, a sequential-state `extract_targets_from_arrays` loop, and a fixed `nan_savgol` degree formula; expanded/corrected `tests/test_targets.py` regressions; regenerated real 4-track artifacts with a quantitative jump-statistic and crop-edge comparison against the pre-fix debug-session measurements.
</objective>
